# Major Defense Contracting Hubs

## Huntsville, Alabama - Redstone Arsenal

Huntsville is one of the most important defense technology hubs in the United States, centered on Redstone Arsenal. The installation hosts the Army Materiel Command (AMC), the Aviation and Missile Command (AMCOM), PEO Missiles and Space, the Missile Defense Agency (MDA), and the Army Contracting Command-Redstone Arsenal (ACC-RSA). ACC-Redstone Arsenal obligated more than $34.2 billion in contract awards by the end of FY2025, making it one of the Army's largest contracting activities.

Major contractors in the Huntsville area include Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, Boeing, Raytheon (RTX), Dynetics (Leidos), and numerous small businesses specializing in missile defense and advanced weapons. The area specializes in missile defense, hypersonic weapons, space launch systems, AI-enabled guidance systems, and precision strike technologies. Key programs include the Long Range Hypersonic Weapon (LRHW), the Common Hypersonic Glide Body (C-HGB), and Terminal High Altitude Area Defense (THAAD). NAICS codes 541715 (R&D) and 336414 (guided missiles) dominate local contracting.

Huntsville's defense heritage dates to the Army Ballistic Missile Agency and the Saturn V rocket program. Today it combines that missile expertise with modern AI, cybersecurity, and advanced manufacturing capabilities, creating a uniquely dense defense technology ecosystem.

## Northern Virginia - Pentagon and Crystal City

The Washington DC metropolitan area, particularly Northern Virginia, is the epicenter of defense contracting. The Pentagon, Fort Belvoir, and numerous contractor offices in Arlington and Crystal City drive billions in annual awards. This area hosts major IT, cybersecurity, and consulting contracts supporting the Office of the Secretary of Defense, the Joint Staff, and numerous defense agencies. GDIT, Leidos, BAE Systems, Booz Allen Hamilton, and SAIC maintain significant presence. Key NAICS codes include 541512 (cybersecurity/IT) and 541614 (consulting).

## Aberdeen, Maryland - Aberdeen Proving Ground

Aberdeen Proving Ground (APG) hosts DEVCOM (Combat Capabilities Development Command), the Army Research Laboratory (ARL), the C5ISR Center, and the Chemical Biological Center (CBC). ARL is the Army's sole fundamental research laboratory, serving as the nexus between the military, academia, and industry. APG focuses on autonomous systems, robotics, AI/ML research, soldier systems, ground vehicle technology, chemical and biological defense, and information technology.

Key recent contracts at APG include ECS's $190 million AI/ML integration contract (2024), Sandtable's $38 million agentic AI contract (2025), and Scale AI's $99 million AI research contract (2025). The installation hosts the Robotics Research Collaboration Campus (R2C2), the Robotic Combat Vehicle (RCV) testing program, and the Human-Machine Integrated Formation (HMIF) networks initiative. ACC-APG manages contracting for research, development, and testing activities across multiple DEVCOM centers.

## San Diego, California - Naval Information Warfare

San Diego is a major Navy hub, home to Naval Information Warfare Systems Command (NAVWAR, formerly SPAWAR). The area focuses on communications, C4ISR, electronic warfare, and maritime systems. Camp Pendleton provides Marine Corps presence. Major contractors include BAE Systems, L3Harris, and Northrop Grumman. San Diego's defense technology sector specializes in secure communications, network-centric warfare, and undersea warfare systems.

## Colorado Springs, Colorado - Space Command

Colorado Springs hosts US Space Command (USSPACECOM), NORAD, Peterson Space Force Base, and Schriever Space Force Base. The area is the center of military space operations, satellite communications, missile warning, and space domain awareness. Key contractors include Lockheed Martin Space, Northrop Grumman, and L3Harris. The region is also home to the Air Force Academy and numerous small space technology companies.

## Dayton, Ohio - Wright-Patterson AFB

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base hosts the Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) and the Air Force Life Cycle Management Center (AFLCMC). It is a primary hub for aerospace R&D, materials science, sensors, and directed energy weapons research. The base manages significant R&D contracting in NAICS 541715 and serves as the Air Force's primary acquisition management center.

## Fort Liberty, North Carolina

Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg) hosts the US Army Special Operations Command (USASOC), XVIII Airborne Corps, and the 82nd Airborne Division. The installation generates approximately $1 billion in annual contracts covering logistics, sustainment, base support, and materiel services. Key programs include the Enhanced Army Global Logistics Enterprise (EAGLE), currently valued at $591 million with Vectrus Systems, and the upcoming EAGLE II program worth up to $913 million. Contracting activity covers special operations equipment, training systems, supply chain services, facility maintenance, and medical logistics at Womack Army Medical Center.

## Tampa, Florida - CENTCOM and SOCOM

MacDill AFB in Tampa hosts both US Central Command (CENTCOM) and US Special Operations Command (SOCOM). The area generates significant contracting in intelligence, special operations technology, and regional security assistance. Contractors include L3Harris, SAIC, Leidos, and various specialized firms supporting overseas operations and intelligence analysis.
