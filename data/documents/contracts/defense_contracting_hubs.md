# Major Defense Contracting Hubs

## Huntsville, Alabama - Redstone Arsenal

Huntsville is one of the most important defense technology hubs in the United States. Redstone Arsenal hosts the Army Materiel Command (AMC), the Aviation and Missile Command (AMCOM), PEO Missiles and Space, and the Missile Defense Agency (MDA). Major contractors in the area include Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, Boeing, and Raytheon. The area specializes in missile defense, hypersonic weapons, and space launch systems. NAICS codes 541715 and 336414 dominate local contracting.

## Northern Virginia - Pentagon and Crystal City

The Washington DC metropolitan area, particularly Northern Virginia, is the epicenter of defense contracting. The Pentagon, Fort Belvoir, and numerous contractor offices in Arlington and Crystal City drive billions in annual awards. This area hosts major IT, cybersecurity, and consulting contracts. GDIT, Leidos, BAE Systems, and Booz Allen Hamilton all maintain significant presence. Key NAICS codes include 541512 (cybersecurity) and 541614 (consulting).

## San Diego, California - Naval Information Warfare

San Diego is a major Navy hub, home to Naval Information Warfare Systems Command (NAVWAR, formerly SPAWAR). The area focuses on communications, C4ISR, electronic warfare, and maritime systems. Camp Pendleton provides Marine Corps presence. Major contractors include BAE Systems, L3Harris, and Northrop Grumman.

## Colorado Springs, Colorado - Space Command

Colorado Springs hosts US Space Command (USSPACECOM), NORAD, Peterson Space Force Base, and Schriever Space Force Base. The area is the center of military space operations, satellite communications, missile warning, and space domain awareness. Key contractors include Lockheed Martin Space, Northrop Grumman, and L3Harris.

## Dayton, Ohio - Wright-Patterson AFB

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base hosts the Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) and the Air Force Life Cycle Management Center (AFLCMC). It is a primary hub for aerospace R&D, materials science, sensors, and directed energy weapons research. The base manages significant R&D contracting in NAICS 541715.

## Aberdeen, Maryland - Aberdeen Proving Ground

Aberdeen Proving Ground (APG) hosts DEVCOM (Combat Capabilities Development Command), the Army Research Laboratory (ARL), and the C5ISR Center. It focuses on soldier systems, ground vehicle technology, chemical and biological defense, and information technology. Major NAICS codes include 541715, 541512, and 541714.

## Tampa, Florida - CENTCOM and SOCOM

MacDill AFB in Tampa hosts both US Central Command (CENTCOM) and US Special Operations Command (SOCOM). The area generates significant contracting in intelligence, special operations technology, and regional security assistance. Contractors include L3Harris, SAIC/Leidos, and various specialized firms.

## Fort Liberty, North Carolina

Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg) hosts the US Army Special Operations Command (USASOC) and XVIII Airborne Corps. Contracting activity focuses on special operations equipment, training systems, parachute and airdrop systems, and tactical communications.
