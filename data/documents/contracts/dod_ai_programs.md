# Department of Defense Artificial Intelligence Programs

## Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office (CDAO)

The Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office (CDAO) leads the Department of Defense's transformation into an AI-first organization. Established by DoD Directive 5105.89 in November 2024, the CDAO is responsible for accelerating DoD adoption of data, analytics, and artificial intelligence from the boardroom to the battlefield. The CDAO was realigned under the Office of the Undersecretary of Defense for Research and Engineering (USD(R&E)) to accelerate department-wide AI transformation.

The CDAO's mission has two parts: advancing deterrence by ensuring warfighters have the best digital capabilities, and beating bureaucracy by ensuring critical business functions have the digital solutions to deliver for warfighters and taxpayers. The office sets foundational policy, guiding the department on roles, responsibilities, and AI integration.

## GenAI.mil Platform

GenAI.mil is the Pentagon's secure, purpose-built platform for generative AI. Launched in December 2025, it began with Google Cloud's Gemini for Government as the first of several planned enterprise AI models. The platform provides nearly 3 million DoD users—military, civilian, and contract employees—with desktop access to AI tools for research, document writing, operations analysis, logistics, cyber defense, and more. All tools are certified for Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) and DoD Impact Level 5, making them operationally approved and secure. By 2026, GenAI.mil reached 1 million unique active users with nearly 40 million prompts entered.

## AI Rapid Capabilities Cell (AI RCC)

Launched in December 2024, the AI Rapid Capabilities Cell is a joint CDAO and Defense Innovation Unit (DIU) initiative to rapidly accelerate generative and frontier AI adoption across the military. The AI RCC replaced Task Force Lima and received $100 million in pilot funding for FY24 and FY25. It focuses on fast, experiment-driven pilots in warfighting areas including command and control (C2), decision support, operational planning, logistics, autonomous systems, and intelligence. Enterprise management applications include HR, finance, supply chain, legal, procurement, healthcare, cybersecurity, and software development. The RCC builds on commercial technology and aims to shorten the adoption timeline from years to months.

## AI Contracts at Aberdeen Proving Ground

DEVCOM Army Research Laboratory (ARL) at Aberdeen Proving Ground, Maryland, is the Army's primary hub for AI research and development. Key contracts awarded in 2024-2025 include:

Sandtable, Inc. received a 5-year, $38 million IDIQ contract from ACC-APG Adelphi in May 2025 to advance and operationalize agentic AI technologies. The contract supports DEVCOM ARL's research, development, and transition of autonomous and semi-autonomous AI systems that can reason, plan, and execute for real-world Army applications.

ECS, a division of ASGN Incorporated, received a 3-year, $190 million contract in 2024 to serve as a system integrator for DEVCOM ARL. ECS delivers and integrates AI capabilities for the Department of Defense, focusing on developing, experimenting with, and demonstrating advanced AI mobility and agility for field-level and theater-level programs.

Scale AI Inc. was awarded a 5-year contract with a ceiling of approximately $99 million in August 2025 by DEVCOM ARL for foundational and applied AI research, covering a broad range of AI support for basic and applied Army research.

Purdue University received a $1.5 million, 5-year award from DEVCOM ARL in late 2025 for developing autonomous air-ground robot teams capable of operating in GPS-denied environments, emphasizing AI collaboration, multi-agent reasoning, and field autonomy.

## Project Linchpin

Project Linchpin is the Army's program to deliver scalable, trusted AI and machine learning capabilities for Army systems. The Army Research Laboratory and ACC-APG Adelphi awarded contracts in 2023-2024 to Booz Allen Hamilton and Red Hat for foundational research, focusing on open architectures and principles for data and model integrity, traceability, and security. The program aims to create modular, open AI systems that can be rapidly deployed across Army formations.

## AI Research at Redstone Arsenal

Redstone Arsenal in Huntsville, Alabama hosts the Army Materiel Command (AMC), the Aviation and Missile Command (AMCOM), PEO Missiles and Space, and the Missile Defense Agency (MDA). AI research at Redstone focuses on intelligent missile guidance systems, predictive maintenance for aviation platforms, and machine learning for logistics optimization. The installation's proximity to major defense contractors including Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, Boeing, and Raytheon creates a dense ecosystem for AI-enabled defense technology development. Army Contracting Command-Redstone Arsenal obligated more than $34.2 billion in contract awards by the end of FY2025.

## SBIR/STTR AI Programs

The Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) programs fund numerous AI-focused defense projects. These programs provide equity-free, non-dilutive funding to American small businesses. As of October 2024, agencies may issue Phase I awards up to $323,090 and Phase II awards up to $2,153,927. Many SBIR/STTR awards at Redstone Arsenal and Aberdeen Proving Ground focus on AI applications including computer vision, natural language processing, autonomous navigation, and predictive analytics for military systems.
