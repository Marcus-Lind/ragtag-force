# DoD Hypersonics Research and Development Programs

## Overview of Military Hypersonics

Hypersonic weapons travel at speeds exceeding Mach 5 and represent one of the Department of Defense's highest modernization priorities. The Army, Navy, and Air Force are all pursuing hypersonic strike capabilities to counter near-peer adversaries. Redstone Arsenal in Huntsville, Alabama serves as the national hub for hypersonics research, development, testing, and evaluation (RDT&E), manufacturing, and deployment.

## Long Range Hypersonic Weapon (LRHW)

The Long Range Hypersonic Weapon is the Army's ground-launched hypersonic missile system, designed to provide strategic long-range precision strike capability. The LRHW system uses the Common Hypersonic Glide Body (C-HGB) as its warhead, mounted on a mobile ground launcher. The program is managed through the Hypersonic Project Office and the Portfolio Acquisition Executive for Fires (PAE FIRES) at Redstone Arsenal.

Lockheed Martin Space, based in Huntsville, Alabama, received a $756.8 million contract modification in May 2024 for ground support equipment and integration support for deploying LRHW batteries and infrastructure, with an estimated completion date of February 2028. The contract was awarded by Army Contracting Command at Redstone Arsenal.

## Common Hypersonic Glide Body (C-HGB)

The Common Hypersonic Glide Body is a joint Army-Navy program that provides the maneuverable warhead component for multiple hypersonic weapon systems. In November 2024, Dynetics, a Leidos subsidiary based in Huntsville, Alabama, was awarded a $670.5 million contract to build the C-HGB and its thermal protection systems. The contract runs through at least October 2029, with $65.8 million from Army FY2024 RDT&E funds obligated at award. Dynetics, along with Lockheed Martin and other partners, is developing and manufacturing the first commercially produced C-HGB sets for integration onto Army LRHW and other platforms.

## Launcher Systems Production

Also in November 2024, Dynetics received a separate $204 million contract for production and delivery of launcher systems, spares, and incidentals for Army hypersonic missile needs. The Army obligated $99.9 million from FY2025 procurement appropriations. Performance locations include Huntsville, Alabama and other sites. The contract was awarded by Army Contracting Command at Redstone Arsenal.

## Hypersonics Research in Alabama

Alabama, particularly the Huntsville metro area around Redstone Arsenal, is the epicenter of US military hypersonics development. The state hosts the Army's hypersonic offices including the Hypersonic Project Office and PAE FIRES, which oversees hypersonic, missile, and long-range fires modernization. Major contractors in the Huntsville area working on hypersonics include Dynetics (Leidos), Lockheed Martin, Raytheon (RTX), Northrop Grumman, and Boeing. The concentration of missile defense expertise dating back to the Saturn V rocket program makes Huntsville uniquely positioned for hypersonic weapons development.

By the end of FY2025, Army Contracting Command-Redstone Arsenal obligated more than $34.2 billion in contract awards, including major production contracts and research-driven programs in precision strike, hypersonics, and future munitions development.

## Raytheon and Hypersonic Air-Breathing Weapons

Raytheon (now RTX) has been a major contractor for hypersonic air-breathing weapon concepts, particularly the Hypersonic Air-breathing Weapon Concept (HAWC) program. These scramjet-powered missiles achieve sustained hypersonic flight and represent an alternative approach to glide body weapons. Raytheon's hypersonic work spans multiple locations but draws heavily on the defense industrial base in Alabama and other southeastern states.

## Missile Defense Agency Programs

The Missile Defense Agency, headquartered at Redstone Arsenal in Huntsville, manages ballistic missile defense programs that are increasingly incorporating hypersonic threat detection and defeat capabilities. MDA programs focus on developing sensors, interceptors, and command and control systems capable of tracking and engaging hypersonic threats. The agency works closely with DARPA on advanced hypersonic defense research.
