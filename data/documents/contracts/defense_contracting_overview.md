# Defense Contracting Overview

## What is Federal Defense Contracting

Federal defense contracting is the process by which the Department of Defense (DoD) acquires goods, services, and research from private sector companies. The DoD is the largest contracting organization in the US government, awarding hundreds of billions of dollars annually across all military service branches.

## Key Agencies

The Department of Defense includes several major contracting agencies:

- **Department of the Army** - Manages Army acquisition programs, including weapons systems, logistics, and research at installations like Redstone Arsenal and Aberdeen Proving Ground.
- **Department of the Navy** - Covers both Navy and Marine Corps procurement, with major activity at NAVWAR in San Diego and various shipyards.
- **Department of the Air Force** - Includes Air Force and Space Force acquisition, with the Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) at Wright-Patterson AFB being a major R&D hub.
- **Defense Logistics Agency (DLA)** - Provides logistics support across all services.
- **Missile Defense Agency (MDA)** - Manages ballistic missile defense programs, headquartered in Huntsville, AL.

## NAICS Codes in Defense

The North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) categorizes businesses by industry. Key NAICS codes in defense contracting include:

- **541715** - Research and Development in Physical, Engineering, and Life Sciences. Covers AI/ML, hypersonics, directed energy, and advanced weapons research.
- **541512** - Computer Systems Design Services. Covers cybersecurity, IT modernization, cloud computing, and network engineering.
- **541330** - Engineering Services. Covers C4ISR systems, radar, electronic warfare, and systems engineering.
- **541614** - Process, Physical Distribution, and Logistics Consulting. Covers supply chain, sustainment, and depot-level maintenance.
- **541714** - Research and Development in Biotechnology. Covers biodefense, CBRN defense, and medical countermeasures.
- **336411** - Aircraft Manufacturing. Covers fighter jets, helicopters, drones, and UAVs.

## Contract Types

Federal contracts come in several pricing structures:

- **Firm Fixed Price (FFP)** - The contractor delivers at a set price regardless of actual costs. Lowest risk for the government.
- **Cost Plus Fixed Fee (CPFF)** - Government reimburses allowable costs plus a fixed fee. Used for R&D where costs are unpredictable.
- **Time and Materials (T&M)** - Government pays hourly rates plus materials. Common for IT services.
- **Indefinite Delivery / Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ)** - Framework contract with task orders. Enables rapid ordering against pre-competed vehicles.

## The Acquisition Process

1. Requirements are defined by the requiring activity
2. Market research identifies potential sources
3. Solicitation is posted (often on SAM.gov)
4. Proposals are evaluated against stated criteria
5. Award is made to the best value offeror
6. Contract is administered through delivery and closeout

## Small Business Programs

The DoD has mandatory small business contracting goals:
- Small Business: 23% of prime contracts
- Small Disadvantaged Business: 5%
- Women-Owned Small Business: 5%
- HUBZone: 3%
- Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned: 3%
