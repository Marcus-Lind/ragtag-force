# Major Defense Contractors and Cybersecurity Programs

## Lockheed Martin Corporation

Lockheed Martin is the largest Department of Defense contractor, receiving $50.7 billion in awards for FY2024—more than double the next competitor. The company operates across all defense domains including aerospace, missiles, cyber, and space systems.

### Cybersecurity and Digital Defense Programs

Lockheed Martin has increasingly focused on multi-domain integration, blending cyber, space, air, land, and sea operations. Key cybersecurity-related contracts in 2024-2025 include:

C2BMC-Next (Command and Control, Battle Management and Communications): Awarded by the Missile Defense Agency in 2024 with a ceiling of $4.1 billion, this contract covers upgrades to the C2BMC system for faster, globally coordinated multi-domain defense operations. The program includes new cybersecurity capabilities, sensor integration, and AI-powered battle management for space domain awareness. The contract runs through 2029 with an option to extend to 2034.

F-35 Lightning II Sustainment: Multiple contracts including a $663 million modification in 2024 and a $1.15 billion site activation contract spanning 2024-2028. These include sustainment of advanced cyber-secure logistics, mission systems, and support for integrated training and digital engineering environments critical for operating in contested, cyber-threatened environments.

Precision Strike Missile (PrSM): Up to $4.94 billion IDIQ contract awarded in 2025 by the US Army. PrSM features digital and open-architecture design for rapid upgrade and secure communications, reflecting the growing emphasis on networked, cyber-resilient weapons systems.

THAAD (Terminal High Altitude Area Defense): A $2.8 billion contract awarded by the Missile Defense Agency in 2025. THAAD uses advanced sensor fusion and integrated network defense to counter missile threats, including cyber-based attacks on sensor and command networks.

### Lockheed Martin Strategy

Lockheed Martin's defense strategy emphasizes open architecture for faster and more secure digital upgrades, AI-driven battle management and cybersecurity essential for modern networked warfare, and 5G.MIL Unified Networks providing secure, resilient connectivity spanning classified and unclassified operations.

## RTX Corporation (Raytheon)

RTX, formerly Raytheon Technologies, is the second-largest defense contractor. The company's defense divisions focus on missiles, radar systems, electronic warfare, and cybersecurity. Raytheon Intelligence and Space provides cyber solutions for military and intelligence customers, including offensive and defensive cyber operations, network security, and secure communications. Key programs include the Hypersonic Air-breathing Weapon Concept (HAWC) and various missile defense systems.

## Northrop Grumman

Northrop Grumman is a major defense contractor specializing in aerospace, electronics, and cybersecurity. The company provides cyber operations platforms, secure communications systems, and space-based intelligence capabilities. Northrop Grumman's cybersecurity work includes protecting classified networks, developing zero-trust architectures for DoD systems, and operating secure cloud environments for intelligence and defense customers.

## Boeing Defense

Boeing Defense, Space and Security provides aircraft, weapons systems, satellites, and cybersecurity solutions. Boeing's cyber capabilities include protecting weapon systems from cyber threats, developing autonomous systems with cyber-resilient architectures, and providing secure satellite communications. The company has significant presence at multiple defense installations including Redstone Arsenal, Alabama.

## General Dynamics Information Technology (GDIT)

GDIT provides IT and cybersecurity services to military and intelligence customers. The company operates and secures some of the Department of Defense's most critical networks and information systems. GDIT contracts cover cloud computing, zero-trust security, identity and access management, and continuous monitoring of defense networks.

## BAE Systems

BAE Systems provides electronic warfare, intelligence systems, and cybersecurity solutions. The company develops cyber-hardened platforms, secure radio communications, and electronic countermeasures. BAE Systems has significant contracts for protecting military platforms from both kinetic and cyber threats.

## Booz Allen Hamilton

Booz Allen Hamilton is a leading defense consulting and cybersecurity firm. The company provides cyber threat intelligence, incident response, vulnerability assessments, and digital forensics for military customers. Booz Allen's defense work includes supporting the Army's Project Linchpin AI program with contracts awarded in 2023-2024 for foundational AI research at Aberdeen Proving Ground.

## Defense Cybersecurity Market

The Department of Defense cybersecurity market encompasses network defense, application security, cloud security, endpoint protection, and offensive cyber operations. DoD cybersecurity spending has increased significantly as the military transitions to cloud-based systems and faces growing threats from nation-state adversaries. The Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC) program requires all defense contractors to meet specific cybersecurity standards, creating additional demand for cyber solutions across the defense industrial base.
