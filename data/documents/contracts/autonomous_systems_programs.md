# Autonomous Systems and Robotics Programs at Aberdeen Proving Ground

## DEVCOM and Aberdeen Proving Ground Overview

Aberdeen Proving Ground (APG) in Maryland is the Army's premier center for autonomous systems research, development, testing, and evaluation. The installation hosts DEVCOM (Combat Capabilities Development Command), the Army Research Laboratory (ARL), the C5ISR Center, and the Chemical Biological Center (CBC). APG focuses on soldier systems, ground vehicle technology, chemical and biological defense, information technology, and increasingly, autonomous systems and robotics.

## Robotic Combat Vehicle (RCV) Program

The Robotic Combat Vehicle program is the Army's flagship autonomous ground platform initiative. In 2024, four vendors—McQ, Textron Systems, General Dynamics Land Systems, and Oshkosh Defense—delivered prototypes to Aberdeen Proving Ground in August for evaluation. The Army conducted extensive testing at APG and was scheduled to select the winning contractor by March 2025 for the next prototyping and requirement phases. The RCV program aims to field unmanned ground vehicles that can operate alongside manned formations, providing reconnaissance, direct fire, and logistics support while reducing risk to soldiers.

## Human-Machine Integrated Formation (HMIF) Networks

On September 13, 2024, the Army's Rapid Capabilities and Critical Technologies Office (RCCTO) held its first HMIF Industry Day at Aberdeen Proving Ground. A Request for Proposals was planned for October 2024 via the National Advanced Mobility Consortium (NAMC), with contract awards anticipated in early FY2025. The HMIF program focuses on robust command-and-control networks for integrated robotic formations, enabling seamless communication between manned and unmanned platforms in contested electromagnetic environments.

## DEVCOM ARL Robotics Research

The Army Research Laboratory at APG operates the Robotics Research Collaboration Campus (R2C2) for multi-domain collaborative autonomy and AI research. R2C2 hosts field trials and technology demonstrations for unmanned ground and aerial systems. ARL's research focuses on autonomous navigation in GPS-denied environments, human-robot teaming, multi-agent coordination, and adaptive autonomy that can operate in complex terrain.

Key research areas include autonomous mobility, air-ground teaming, unmanned platform behaviors, AI-enabled object tracking, resilient drone operations, and human-machine teaming. ARL actively solicits industry and academic partnerships for concept demonstration and evaluation at Aberdeen.

## Chemical Biological Defense Autonomy

The DEVCOM Chemical Biological Center (CBC) at APG works on autonomous decontamination technology, AI and machine learning integration for sensors, and advanced robotics for chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear (CBRN) defense. Projects include the Autonomous Equipment Decontamination System and unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) bio-sensor integration. These autonomous CBRN systems are designed to detect, identify, and neutralize hazards without exposing soldiers to dangerous environments.

## C5ISR Autonomous Systems Integration

The C5ISR Center at Aberdeen Proving Ground integrates autonomous systems with command, control, communications, computers, cyber, intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance capabilities. In November 2024, Veritech received a sole-source contract to support DEVCOM C5ISR at Aberdeen, focusing on power and data solutions for tactical communications that enable integrated autonomous systems operations.

## Industry Engagement at APG

Aberdeen Proving Ground regularly hosts Advance Planning Briefings for Industry (APBI) to outline future contracting opportunities in robotics, command and control, testing, and chemical-biological defense for upcoming fiscal years. The 2024 APBI occurred in April, with explicit focus on DEVCOM, ARL, C5ISR, and chemical-biological applications. These events help industry partners understand the Army's autonomous systems requirements and compete for research and development contracts.

## Autonomous Systems Testing and Evaluation

APG serves as a critical testing ground for autonomous systems due to its extensive range facilities, diverse terrain, and proximity to DEVCOM laboratories. The installation provides controlled environments for testing autonomous navigation, obstacle avoidance, target recognition, and multi-vehicle coordination. Testing at APG validates that autonomous systems meet Army performance requirements before fielding to operational units.
