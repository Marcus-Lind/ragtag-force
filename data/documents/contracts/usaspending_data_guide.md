# USAspending Data Guide for Defense Analysis

## What is USAspending.gov

USAspending.gov is the official source for federal spending data. It tracks all federal awards including contracts, grants, loans, and other financial assistance. The data is updated regularly and goes back to fiscal year 2008. For defense contracting, it provides detailed information about who received contracts, for what purpose, where work was performed, and how much was awarded.

## Searching for Defense Contracts

When searching for defense contracts, the most useful filters include:

### By Agency
All military contracts fall under the toptier agency "Department of Defense." Subtier agencies include Department of the Army, Department of the Navy, Department of the Air Force, Defense Logistics Agency, Missile Defense Agency, and others.

### By NAICS Code
NAICS codes categorize the type of work performed. For defense R&D, code 541715 covers physical and engineering sciences research. For IT and cybersecurity, code 541512 covers computer systems design. For engineering services including C4ISR, code 541330 applies.

### By Location
Contracts can be filtered by place of performance, showing where work actually happens. This is critical for understanding which installations and regions receive the most investment.

### By Contractor
The recipient name field allows searching for specific companies. Major defense contractors like Lockheed Martin, RTX (Raytheon), Northrop Grumman, Boeing, and General Dynamics routinely appear in the top recipients.

## Award Types

USAspending categorizes contract awards using type codes:
- **A** - BPA Call (Blanket Purchase Agreement Call)
- **B** - Purchase Order
- **C** - Delivery Order (against an IDIQ or other indefinite contract)
- **D** - Definitive Contract (standalone contract award)

For most defense analysis, searching types A through D covers all contract vehicles.

## Understanding Award Amounts

Award amounts in USAspending represent the total obligated value over the life of the contract, not just a single year. A contract awarded in 2020 with modifications through 2024 will show the cumulative total. For analyzing annual spending, filter by the time period of interest.

## Key Metrics for Analysis

When analyzing defense spending, consider:
- **Total obligations** - How much has been committed to a program or area
- **Number of awards** - How many contracts were issued
- **Top recipients** - Which companies received the most funding
- **Geographic distribution** - Where the money is going
- **NAICS concentration** - What types of work dominate
