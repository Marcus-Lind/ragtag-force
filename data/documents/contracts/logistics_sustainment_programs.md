# Army Logistics and Sustainment Contracting

## Fort Liberty Overview

Fort Liberty, formerly known as Fort Bragg, is located in North Carolina and hosts the US Army Special Operations Command (USASOC), XVIII Airborne Corps, and the 82nd Airborne Division. The installation is one of the Army's largest and most active posts, generating approximately $1 billion in logistics, sustainment, base support, and materiel contracts annually. Fort Liberty serves as a critical hub for rapid deployment logistics, special operations support, and sustainment operations.

## EAGLE Program (Enhanced Army Global Logistics Enterprise)

The Enhanced Army Global Logistics Enterprise (EAGLE) is one of the Army's largest logistics support programs. Vectrus Systems has held the EAGLE contract at Fort Liberty since 2016, with the contract value increasing from $546 million to $591 million and extensions running through June 2027. The contract covers materiel maintenance, supply, ammunition, transportation, and logistics management at the Logistics Readiness Center (LRC), supporting the 406th Army Field Support Battalion and broader Army Sustainment Command (ASC) requirements.

The Army issued a solicitation for EAGLE II, a total small business set-aside covering sustainment, field maintenance, supply services, and transportation. This follow-on program is expected to cover up to $913 million in potential logistics support requirements, representing significant opportunity for the Fort Liberty supply chain community in 2024 and 2025.

## Recent Logistics Contracts at Fort Liberty

Technica LLC was awarded a $21.7 million cost-plus-fixed-fee contract in December 2024 for logistics support services at Fort Liberty. The contract supports Army sustainment and supply chain operations through December 2029, funded by FY2025 Army operation and maintenance funds. The contracting activity was the US Army Contracting Command at Rock Island Arsenal, Illinois.

Vanquish Worldwide was selected in November 2024 to deliver Supply Support Activity (SSA) assistance for Alpha Company, 189th Division Sustainment Support Battalion (DSSB). The contract covers receipt, storage, and issue of various supply classes for XVIII Airborne Corps and the 82nd Airborne Division, spanning 413 storage locations. This supports Echelons Above Brigade units for ongoing training, deployment, sustainment, and redeployment efforts.

Valiant received a $41 million four-year contract in November 2023 for facility modernization, operations, and maintenance at Womack Army Medical Center (WAMC) at Fort Liberty, including repair, facility management, logistics, and sustainment support.

## Army Supply Chain Modernization

The Army is modernizing its supply chain through several initiatives. The Army Logistics Enterprise Integration is replacing legacy systems with modern cloud-based platforms for inventory management, demand forecasting, and distribution optimization. These programs leverage AI and machine learning to predict equipment failures, optimize spare parts positioning, and reduce supply chain disruptions. Fort Liberty, as a power projection platform for XVIII Airborne Corps, is a priority installation for supply chain modernization due to its rapid deployment mission.

## Special Operations Logistics

Fort Liberty hosts the US Army Special Operations Command, which has unique logistics requirements for specialized equipment, training systems, parachute and airdrop systems, and tactical communications. Special operations logistics contracts at Fort Liberty cover everything from specialized weapons maintenance to custom equipment fabrication. The special operations community also drives innovation in lightweight logistics, autonomous resupply, and expeditionary sustainment systems.

## Defense Logistics Agency Support

The Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) provides logistics support across all services, including significant operations at Fort Liberty. DLA manages supply chains for consumable items, fuel, repair parts, and other materials essential to base operations. DLA contracts at Fort Liberty cover food services, clothing and textiles, industrial hardware, and medical supplies, representing a major portion of the installation's annual contracting activity.

## Sustainment Training and Readiness

The 189th DSSB and other sustainment units at Fort Liberty maintain readiness through continuous training exercises and real-world deployment operations. Sustainment contracts support both garrison operations and deployment preparation, ensuring units can rapidly transition from home station to forward operating locations with full logistics capability.
